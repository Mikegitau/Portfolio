Skills

The Most Important Skills to Develop to Succeed in Life

  • Communication and interpersonal skills
  • Decision-making and problem-solving
  • Creative thinking and critical thinking
  • Self-awareness and empathy
  • Assertiveness
  • Resilience and ability to cope with problems

Communication and interpersonal skills

This broadly describes the skills needed to get on and work with other people, and particularly to transfer and receive messages either in writing or verbally.

Decision-making and problem-solving

This describes the skills required to understand problems, find solutions to them, alone or with others, and then take action to address them.

Creative thinking and critical thinking

This describes the ability to think in different and unusual ways about problems, and find new solutions, or generate new ideas, coupled with the ability to assess information carefully and understand its relevance.

Self-awareness and empathy

They describe understanding yourself and being able to feel for other people as if their experiences were happening to you.

Assertiveness

These describe the skills needed to stand up for yourself and other people, and remain calm even in the face of considerable provocation.

Resilience and ability to cope with problems

which describes the ability to recover from setbacks, and treat them as opportunities to learn, or simply experiences.
